# Literature Notes — HelveVista

Key references and their relevance to the prototype.

---

## Core DSR Methodology

**Hevner, A. R., March, S. T., Park, J., & Ram, S. (2004)**
Design science in information systems research.
*MIS Quarterly*, 28(1), 75–105.

Relevance: Defines the methodological framework. The prototype is the IT artifact.
Key concepts: utility, rigor, design cycle, evaluation.

---

## Coordination Theory

**Malone, T. W., & Crowston, K. (1994)**
The interdisciplinary study of coordination.
*ACM Computing Surveys*, 26(1), 87–119.

Relevance: Theoretical foundation for the coordination problem.
The Stellenwechsel = classic inter-organizational coordination challenge.
Key concepts: dependencies, coordination mechanisms, actors.

---

## Agentic AI

**Hosseini, S., & Seilani, H. (2025)**
The role of agentic AI in shaping a smart future.
*Array.*

Relevance: Copilot vs. Autopilot taxonomy.
HelveVista targets the Autopilot category — proactive, multilateral orchestration.

---

## Human-in-the-Loop

**Akbar, F., & Conlan, O. (2024)**
Towards integrating human-in-the-loop control in proactive intelligent personalised agents.
*UMAP.*

Relevance: HITL design principles. Informs the CONFLICT_DETECTED → HITL_REQUIRED mechanism.
Key insight: HITL must be designed to maintain system liveness, not block it.

---

## Pension Literacy

**PLV UZH (2024)**
Pension Literacy Studie Schweiz.

Relevance: Empirical basis for the competency gap that HelveVista addresses.
Supports H3 (user comprehension improvement).

---

## Statistical Data

**BSV / BFS (2024)**
Statistik der AHV 2023.
Statistisches Jahrbuch der Schweiz.

Relevance: Quantitative context for the scale of the problem.
2.546M recipients, CHF 50B expenditure.
